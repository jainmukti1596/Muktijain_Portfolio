Situation

A market leader under pressure

I manage digital marketing for a top-5 European two-wheeler brand across 7 markets at Publicis Groupe, overseeing €1M+ in annual ad spend. Between 2023 and early 2024, the business was performing strongly and the accounts were scaling well.

Then in Q2 2025, everything changed. The brand faced a significant financial downturn — driven by the Euro 5+ regulatory transition, high inflation across European markets, and declining consumer spending. The result was a mid-year budget cut of 35–50% across all accounts. Overnight, the question shifted from "how do we grow?" to "how do we survive this without losing ground?"

"The easy answer would have been to scale everything down proportionally and accept worse results. I didn't want to do that."
Task

Maintain performance with half the budget

My challenge was to protect lead volume and campaign efficiency across our three core markets — Italy, France, and Spain — with drastically reduced budgets. The accounts couldn't just be trimmed. They needed to be fundamentally rethought.

This meant making fast, high-stakes strategic decisions with limited room for error. Every euro had to work harder than it did before.

Action

Three strategic decisions

Rather than a uniform scale-down, I took three deliberate actions — each with a clear rationale tied to efficiency, not just cost-cutting.

1
Rolled out Performance Max across all accounts
PMax was only running in select accounts before the cut. With reduced budgets, I needed Google's AI to work harder — consolidating signals across Search, Display, YouTube, and Discover simultaneously.
Why it mattered: PMax pools conversion data across all placements, which means the bidding algorithm trains faster and makes smarter spend decisions — critical when you have less data to work with due to lower volume.
2
Consolidated fragmented campaign structures
Before the cuts, multiple campaigns were splitting both budget and conversion data. I merged them into consolidated structures to give Smart Bidding a richer signal pool to learn from.
Why it mattered: Smart Bidding needs volume to learn. Fragmented campaigns starve it of data. Consolidation meant the same conversions now powered a single, smarter algorithm instead of several weaker ones.
3
Shifted to brand and model-specific keywords
I removed expensive generic keywords and doubled down on brand and model-level search terms — the kind that signal real, near-purchase intent from serious buyers.
Why it mattered: Generic keywords are costly, competitive, and attract browsers. Brand and model-specific terms are cheaper, higher-intent, and convert at a significantly better rate — exactly what you need when every euro counts.

Spent less. Performed better.

Learnings

What this taught me

Key takeaways from this project
Budget constraints force strategic clarity — the cuts made us eliminate waste we should have removed earlier.
Campaign consolidation is underused. Most accounts benefit from fewer, smarter campaigns rather than more granular ones.
Performance Max works best when it has rich conversion data — consolidation and PMax together compound the benefit.
Intent matters more than volume. High-intent keywords at lower CPC outperform generic keywords at scale when budgets are tight.
The best time to restructure an account is before a crisis. The second best time is during one.
